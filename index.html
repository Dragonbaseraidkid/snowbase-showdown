❄️ Official Rules

Snowbase Showdown

Fast · Fair · Boss-Proof Snow Game

Overview

Snowbase Showdown is a fast, friendly snowball battle where everyone has a base, everyone shares the same ammo station, and scoring is fair and simple.

The game is designed so no one can control all the rules. Rounds are short, snowballs are soft, and the focus is on fun, movement, and teamwork.

2–6 Players Ages ~7+ Snow Required Low Equipment

Setup

1. Bases

Each player (or team) builds a small snow base:

  • Knee-height or lower is enough.
  • Just a short wall to duck behind, not a giant fort.
  • Bases should be spaced out but still within throwing distance.

2. Ammo Station

Build one shared Ammo Station in the middle area:

  • All snowballs come from this pile.
  • No personal snowball stashes or pockets.
  • This makes the game fair and keeps players moving.

3. Play Area

  • Choose an open, safe area with soft snow.
  • No ice patches, roads, or dangerous obstacles.
  • Agree on an outer boundary everyone stays inside.

Scoring

Each gentle snowball hit is worth points:

Hit on jacket or torso
+1 point
Hit on legs
+1 point
Hit on hat
+2 points
Soft, tag-style hit on back
+3 points

First player or team to reach 20 points wins the match.

Safety rule: no aiming at faces or heads on purpose. If a head hit happens by accident, check that the other player is okay, apologize, and keep playing.

Core Rules

1. Starting the Round

  • All players begin behind their own base.
  • Someone calls: “3… 2… 1… Snowbase!”
  • No throwing until after the call finishes.

2. Ammo Rules

  • Players may only collect snowballs from the shared Ammo Station.
  • You may carry a maximum of two snowballs at once (unless a special mode is active).
  • No camping at the Ammo Station—grab and go.

3. Base Rules

  • Your base is a safe zone: you can’t be hit while fully behind it.
  • You also may not throw from inside your base—step out to attack.
  • If a snowball hits your base, it doesn’t count as a point.

4. After a Hit

  • If you hit someone, you call out your points (for example, “1 point!”).
  • Then you must return to your base before throwing again.
  • Other players help keep score honestly and fairly.

Special Modes

1. Base Rush

Every 30–45 seconds, any player may call: “Base Rush!”

  • For the next 5 seconds, bases are not safe.
  • Hits on players at their bases now count for points.
  • After 5 seconds, bases return to being safe zones.

2. Double Ammo Time

Once in a while (e.g., every 60 seconds) someone calls: “Double Ammo!”

  • For the next 10 seconds, players may carry up to four snowballs.
  • After that, everyone returns to the normal 2-snowball limit.

3. Blizzard

Occasionally, a player calls: “Blizzard!”

  • All players must crouch, duck, or hide behind their base for 5 seconds.
  • No throwing and no moving during the blizzard.
  • After 5 seconds, someone calls: “Blizzard over!” and play continues.

Winning & Variations

Standard game: first team or player to reach 20 points wins.

Tie Breaker

  • If two players or teams reach 20 points in the same round, run a 1-on-1 showdown.
  • Both start at their bases, sprint to the Ammo Station, and first hit wins.

Quick Match

  • Play to 10 points for a shorter game.
  • Great for when it’s very cold or getting dark soon.

Co-op Mode

  • Everyone versus a single “Snow Boss” player with extra health (e.g., 40 points).
  • The group works together to reach the boss’s health total with hits.

Safety & Fair Play

Safety first: Snowbase Showdown should always be played with soft snow, gentle throws, and no ice or dangerous objects mixed in. If anyone says “stop” or “time out,” the game pauses immediately.
  • Throw gently—this is not a power contest.
  • No ice chunks, rocks, or hard objects in snowballs.
  • No aiming at faces on purpose.
  • If someone falls or gets hurt, scoring stops until they’re okay.
  • Be honest about hits and points—fair play beats winning.
